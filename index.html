Destination as a Service ‚Äî The Framework
1

Content Layer (DMO-Led)

Authoritative, structured, specific destination content. Schema markup, clear headers, well-organized meta tags ‚Äî "geo" (Generative Engine Optimization). Unique content with specific origins, not top-10 lists. Infrequently asked questions are the new SEO currency.

DMOs make destination data available via APIs and MCP servers (Model Context Protocol) so traveler AI tools can pull accurate, real-time information. Building Your DMO's AI Strategy: A Five-Part Blueprint
JJanette Roush, SVP Innovation & Chief AI Officer

During the Q&A, one partner asked: "Who has an AI strategy?" The room went quiet. Every organization that spoke confirmed they're experimenting ‚Äî yet none have a formal strategy. This blueprint turns the session's insights and your peer feedback into a five-part framework you can take home and build from.

Where DMOs Are Right Now

The gap: Most DMOs are using AI for internal productivity ‚Äî drafting copy, summarizing research, cleaning up meeting notes. These are valuable efficiency gains. Yet almost no one is thinking about AI as a distribution channel for their destination ‚Äî which is the actual strategic shift happening right now.

When the web emerged in the late '90s, DMOs that treated it as "just a brochure online" fell behind. DMOs that treat AI as "just a chatbot" will fall behind the same way. AI is a new distribution model for your content.

A DMO AI Strategy Answers Five Questions in Order

What are we allowed to do? ‚Üí How do we build skill? ‚Üí How do we prove value? ‚Üí How do we get discovered? ‚Üí How do we become the source of truth?
1

Policy ‚Äî The Guardrails

What are we allowed to do?

Most DMOs either have this or are building it. It answers: what tools are approved? What data can go into them? Who has licenses? Visit Orlando's model ‚Äî approved platform, universal access, "no bring your own AI" rule, dedicated trainer ‚Äî is the gold standard from this room. If you have a policy, you've completed step one. If you have a policy and think you have a strategy, you have step one of five.

  • Approved tools list: Which AI platforms can staff use? (ChatGPT, Copilot, Perplexity, Claude, etc.)
  • Data boundaries: What information can go into AI tools? What stays internal?
  • License plan: Who gets paid access? Start with a pilot group, track time saved, use results to justify expansion.
  • BYOAI decision: Can staff use personal AI accounts for work? Orlando says no ‚Äî clarity matters either way.

Metrics & Board Communication ‚Äî The Reporting Problem

How do we prove value when the old metrics only go down?

This came up repeatedly and urgently. DMOs are being evaluated on website traffic ‚Äî a metric that only declines as AI overviews reduce click-throughs. Multiple partners raised this as a governance and funding risk. The strategy: reframe the narrative before the board draws its own conclusions.

  • Stop reporting vanity metrics without context. Website traffic alone tells an incomplete story. If you must report it, pair it with the explanation of why it's declining industry-wide.
  • Add AI-era metrics alongside traditional ones. Visit Orlando tracks AI citations ‚Äî how often the destination appears in AI-generated responses. Easy to pull, and boards find it compelling.
  • Reframe the website's value to your board. The website's job is now dual: serve visitors AND feed AI systems. Traffic is one measure; discoverability in AI responses is another. The website is infrastructure.
  • Prepare a board-ready narrative: "Our website traffic is declining because Google and AI tools answer questions before users click through. Our content is working ‚Äî it's being cited in AI responses. Here's how we're adapting."
  • Agree on new KPIs as a team. What does success look like now? Get cross-functional alignment before presenting to leadership.

4

Content & Discoverability ‚Äî The Website's Second Job

How do we ensure AI systems cite our destination accurately?

Your website still matters ‚Äî but it has a second job: being readable and citable by AI systems. AI doesn't care about your ad budget. It cares about whether your content is specific, authoritative, well-structured, and corroborated by credible third parties.

  • Audit your website schema. Are headers logical? Images tagged? Metadata complete? Use the AI Vision Simulator at jeanetterausch.com to see your site the way AI sees it.
  • Shift from "top 10 lists" to "infrequently asked questions." Generic content is now commoditized. Unique, specific, authoritative content gets cited by AI. Two hundred other sites have the same "Top 10 Things to Do" ‚Äî yours will be invisible.
  • Invest in earned media as an AI investment. Third-party coverage in credible outlets is a credibility signal that AI systems weight heavily. If Town & Country or a respected travel journalist writes about your destination, that carries far more weight in AI responses than your own content. PR budget = AI visibility budget.
  • Optimize for GEO (Generative Engine Optimization). Schema markup, structured data, clear meta tags ‚Äî everything that helps AI read and categorize your content. This is the new SEO.
  • Be patient on AI visibility measurement. Tools claiming to measure AI visibility are largely unreliable today. The metrics will come when OpenAI's ad business needs to prove performance ‚Äî just like Google's did. Focus on fundamentals you can control.

Data Infrastructure ‚Äî Destination as a Service
